## Emergency Procedures - Fire

### Fire Detection
Snowbird is fitted with smoke detectors throughout the house.

### Fire Exits
Please familiarise yourself with your nearest Fire Exit:
* **Top Floor Exit**: at the exterior sliding door located at the bottom of the stairs
* **Entry Floor Exit**: at the front entry or from any of the sliding doors (lounge, bedrooms)
* **Lower Floor Exit**: from the garage door or the exterior sliding door at the top of the stairs

### Action in Case of Fire

#### If You Discover a Fire:
1. **Remain calm**
2. **Touch your door** - If it's hot, do not open it
3. **Exit via the nearest fire exit**
4. **If there is smoke** - crawl beneath it to the nearest exit
5. **Once outside** - proceed immediately to the assembly area at the bottom of the driveway
6. **Call 111** to alert the fire brigade
7. **Do not re-enter** the building for any reason

#### If You Are Trapped in a Room:
1. **Stay calm**
2. **Fill the bathroom sink with water**
3. **Wet sheets and towels** - use them to seal around the door
4. **Call 111** immediately and tell them your exact location in the house

## Property Hazards

### Important Safety Warnings
Please be aware of these potential hazards on the property:

* **Driveway**: Blind spots when entering/exiting - drive slowly and carefully
* **Garden Areas**: Unfenced drop-off areas require caution, especially with children
* **Weather Conditions**: Stairs and driveway can become icy in winter conditions
* **Hot Surfaces**: BBQ, wood fire, and hot water systems - exercise caution
* **Bathtubs**: Slippery when wet - use grab rails
* **Children**: Constant adult supervision required near all potential danger zones

## Gas Safety

* **Gas Shutoff Location**: RHS of entrance stairwell, garage side
* **If you smell gas**: 
  1. Do not use electrical switches or flames
  2. Ventilate the area immediately
  3. Turn off gas at the main shutoff valve
  4. Call emergency services if necessary
  5. Contact property management immediately
